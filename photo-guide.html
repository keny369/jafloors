Homepage

View page

The homepage has four sector card images. These are the first real photographs a visitor sees, so they set the tone for the entire site. Each one should show a finished commercial environment that is clearly identifiable as the relevant sector.

Placeholder: "Healthcare & Aged Care"

600 x 280px (landscape, cropped tight)

A finished healthcare or aged care corridor, treatment room or common area with sheet vinyl flooring visible. The environment should read as clinical or care-adjacent. Avoid empty rooms. A corridor with natural light, visible coved skirting, and a sense of a working care facility is ideal.

Placeholder: "Commercial Kitchens"

600 x 280px (landscape, cropped tight)

A commercial kitchen floor, back-of-house area or service corridor. Resin or safety surface visible. Ideally shows drains, coving or clean wall junctions. Should feel operational, not staged. Equipment in frame is fine.

Placeholder: "Office Fit-outs"

600 x 280px (landscape, cropped tight)

A finished office fit-out showing carpet tile, LVT or polished concrete in a commercial office setting. Corridor, workstation area or reception are all suitable. Should look like a space a tenant would walk into and feel confident about.

Placeholder: "Clean Rooms & Labs"

600 x 280px (landscape, cropped tight)

A controlled environment, laboratory or clean room with seamless flooring visible. Coving details, clean wall transitions, and a sense of precision. If a real clean room image is not available, a pharmaceutical prep area or lab space works.

Post-processing requirements

All images need consistent post-processing before they go on the site. A random mix of phone photos with different colour casts, exposure levels and cropping will undermine the design regardless of how good the layout is. The full set needs to feel like it was shot for this site, even if the images were taken months apart on different jobs with different cameras.

The processing treatment should include:

White balance correction so colours are neutral and consistent across different site conditions and artificial/natural lighting mixes
Slight desaturation to avoid oversaturated oranges and blues that look cheap on screen and clash with the muted brand palette
Shadow lift gently so dark areas have detail without looking flat or washed out
Light grain applied uniformly to tie together images from different sources
Vertical and horizontal straightening which is critical for flooring and construction photography where walls, edges and floor lines must be true
Consistent cropping to the aspect ratios listed above for each slot
Spot removal for any distracting debris, rubbish or personal items in frame

The target feel is clean, competent, natural. Not heavily filtered, not Instagram-warm, not desaturated to the point of looking grey. The images should feel like a capable trade professional documented their work clearly, and then someone who understands visual consistency made the set look like it belongs together.

If you are shooting on a phone: use the highest quality setting, avoid digital zoom, shoot in good natural light where possible, and keep the phone level. Vertical lines that lean inward because the phone was tilted look amateur in any context, but especially in construction and flooring photography where straight lines are the entire subject.
